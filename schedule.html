Creating Sustainable Packaging

9:00 AM – 10:30 AM

With a new public focus on limiting the use of single-use plastics, how can designers begin to design products that are made to be possible to throw away? This panel will discuss alternative approaches to packaging design that focus not only on aesthetics, but on sustainability and biodegradability as well.

Leyla Acaroglu
Eben Bayer
William McDonough

Building Recycling Infrastructure

11:00 AM – 12:00 PM

Recycling and composting services are expanding, but they are still not available to all. Additionally, many recycling programs are inefficient and overburdened. How can we provide everyone with access to these services?

Mike Biddle
Tim Brown
Janette Sadik-Khan

Lunch

Making Green Intuitive

1:30 PM – 3:00 PM

It’s easier to make green choices when there are cues that allow and even encourage people to do so. Hinting at and incentivizing “green” behaviors is yet another way that design can foster sustainability. How do we create products and spaces that encourage green behavior?

Chappell Ellinson
Masamichi Udigawa and Sigi Moeslinger

Spreading Sustainability

3:30 PM – 5:00 PM

Reducing waste and promoting more environmentally-friendly habits are largely dependent upon mobilizing large groups of people. There has been a push to eliminate single-use plastic straws, but change must be enacted on a greater scale. How can we have productive conversations on sustainability with large groups of people?

Andrew Blum
Christine Figgener
Richard Saul Wurman

Networking Dinner Reception

5:00PM – 8:00 PM

Enjoy refreshments and dinner provided by American University Dining, and take time to speak with other attendees and speakers. Please note any food allergies or dietary restrictions.
